    GDP and GNI

    Little John’s shop generates $100,000 in revenue from local customers and $50,000 from international tourists. How would these revenues impact Vietnam’s Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and Gross National Income (GNI)?

    Which of the following statements is correct?

    Answer: Point A

    Explanation:

    GDP measures the total value of goods and services produced within a country’s borders, so the $100,000 from local customers and the $50,000 from international tourists both contribute to Vietnam’s GDP. However, GNI includes the total income earned by residents of a country, including income from abroad, but excludes income earned by foreign entities within the country. Since the $50,000 revenue from international tourists is considered foreign income earned by a local business, it contributes to Vietnam’s GDP but does not directly affect GNI in this context.
