    Production Possibilities

    Now that Little John and you have taken a stroll through the Hanoian market of pho, it’s time to decide on a quantity of pho to produce. To do so, you decide to perform some analysis on the production possibilities curve of Little John’s pho store:

    • You use one day’s worth of money to buy ingredients and produce only pho. Your store manages to produce 100 bowls

    • You repeated the first step but for the second day, you made only beef fried rice (FYI: fried rice and pho are often sold together in Vietnam). 80 dishes it is.

    • You plot these quantities on a graph and obtain this

    Which point should your store produce at?

    Answer: Point E

    Explanation:

    From your knowledge of PPC, point A is out of the question since you won’t have enough ingredients to produce these quantities. At point C, you are wasting some ingredients because more of both pho and fried rice can be produced. Well, it’s quite obvious that you should produce at a point on the PPC, that is, B, C or D. But which point though? It’s always good to have some degree of diversification, since you might devote all your ingredients to producing pho (at B), and on that day the number of customers craving for fried rice is unusually high. In that case you would make a loss. The same goes for point D. Thus, point E - where a mix of both pho and fried rice are produced - is the point you should be heading for.
