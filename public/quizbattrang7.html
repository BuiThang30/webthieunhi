    Price Elasticity of Supply

    If the price of your pottery increases and you want to produce more to gain larger profits, but you find it difficult to quickly increase production, this means that your supply is:

    Answer: Point B

    Explanation:

    In this case, your supply is price inelastic because the quantity supplied does not increase proportionally to price increases. This is typical of handmade goods produced by artisan households on a small scale where production cannot be easily ramped up due to the immobility of resources. It is no easy matter to hire more labor, or to force other family members to get involved in the production line.
