    Price elasticity of demand

    To investigate customers’ reaction to an increase in foods’ price, you did a survey on 2000 consumers chosen randomly. The question of the survey is “Would you buy fresh cốm in Mrs. Phuong store if the price is increased by 20%?”. The result shows that 302 people said no and the rest said yes. Should you increase the price?

    Answer: Point D

    Explanation:

    The demand is actually quite inelastic, as a 20% increase in price only leads to a 15% decrease in customers. Intuitively, it seems to be quite a good deal to increase the price, but actually, 1.2 x 0.85 = 1.02 - this only results in a 2% increase in total revenues. Moreover, the survey just asks about whether or not they will continue to buy fresh cốm here, without considering the amount or quantity. Finally, competitors could decrease their products’ price to attract our customers during this event. Cumulatively, although economics theories may suggest otherwise, there are a lot of risks in realities where we can not hold ceteris paribus. This wise decision will save you from losing a lot of customers.
