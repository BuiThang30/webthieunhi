    Inflation/ Deflation/ Disinflation

    Little John owns a pho shop and is reviewing his finances for the year. Last year, the cost of ingredients for making one bowl of pho was $10. This year, the cost has increased to $12 per bowl. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) for this year is 120, while last year's CPI was 100.

    Using this information, what is the inflation rate for the cost of ingredients in Little John's pho shop?

    Answer: Point A

    Explanation:

    To calculate the inflation rate using the CPI:

    • Calculate the percentage increase in CPI:

      Inflation Rate = (CPI This Year − CPI Last Year ) / CPI Last Year× 100

    • Substituting the values:

      Inflation Rate= (120−100)×100 = 20%

    • This shows the general inflation rate based on the CPI.
