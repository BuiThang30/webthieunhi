    Aggregate Supply

    Little John has recently encountered the following changes:

    • The farms he sourced his ingredient from implemented advanced inventory management software, which reduces waste and improves supply chain efficiency.

    • A sudden increase in the price of raw materials, such as meat and vegetables, has raised his production costs.

    Which aspects of aggregate supply are most directly impacted by these changes?

    Answer: Point C

    Explanation:

    • Short-Run Aggregate Supply (SRAS) represents the total quantity of goods and services that firms are willing to produce at different price levels in the short run. The increase in raw material prices raises production costs, which may decrease SRAS.

    • Long-Run Aggregate Supply (LRAS) is concerned with the economy’s total productive capacity in the long run and is influenced by factors such as technology, labour, and capital rather than short-term changes. As John’s software is a form of technological improvement, it may increase LRAS

    • Natural Rate of Output refers to the economy’s sustainable level of output in the long run, which is less affected by short-term changes in production costs or efficiency.
