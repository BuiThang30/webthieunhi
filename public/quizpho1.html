    Types of market

    The first thing to do when opening the store is to explore the market you are expanding in. To do so, you and Little John hop on a motorbike, ride around the city of Hanoi, and try as many pho stores as you can find. And this pattern occurs:

    • Customer A is craving for a bowl of pho but does not bring any money to buy

    • The flavour of each store is different. Some go with a lighter broth, while others adopt a heavier, meatier kind of broth.

    What type of market you and Little John are expanding into?

    Answer: Point B

    Explanation:

    It is a monopolistic competition as there are many many stores and there is no barrier to entry - Little John can start his business as long as he wants to. The firms - the pho stores in this case - have a degree of market power, enabling them to increase their prices on the basis of their perceived pho quality, which is a mode of non-price competition.
