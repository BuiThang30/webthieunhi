    Price Elasticity of Demand:

    Youâ€™re considering increasing the prices of your handcrafted pottery due to rising production costs. If the demand for your pottery is highly price elastic, what is likely to happen if you raise the prices?

    Answer: Point A

    Explanation:

    For a good with price-elastic demand, its quantity demand is relatively responsive to a change in price. Thus, a decrease in price leads to a more than proportionate increase in quantity demanded, and the loss in revenues due to lower prices is more than made up for by gain in revenues due to higher quantity demanded. Your overall revenue thus increases.
