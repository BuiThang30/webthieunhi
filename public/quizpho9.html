    Aggregate Demand

    Over the past few months, Little John has observed the following economic changes:

    • The local government has recently increased its spending on infrastructure projects, leading to higher local employment and increased disposable income among residents.

    • There has been a noticeable rise in consumer confidence, which has encouraged residents to spend more on dining out. Little John notices this due to the sustained high revenues at his pho shop.

    • Concurrently, there has been a slight increase in interest rates set by the central bank to combat potential inflation.

    Considering these economic changes, which of the following statements accurately reflects the likely impact on aggregate demand for the local economy, and potentially Little Johnâ€™s pho shop?

    Answer: Point A

    Explanation:

    • Aggregate Demand refers to the total demand for goods and services within an economy. Increased government spending and higher consumer confidence both contribute positively to aggregate demand by increasing overall spending.

    • The rise in interest rates can dampen aggregate demand to some extent, as higher interest rates generally lead to higher borrowing costs, which can reduce consumer spending and investment. However, the impact of increased government spending and consumer confidence is likely to be significant enough to still result in a net increase in aggregate demand, although it may not be as large as it would be without the interest rate increase.
